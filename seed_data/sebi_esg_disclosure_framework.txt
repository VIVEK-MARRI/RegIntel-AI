SECURITIES AND EXCHANGE BOARD OF INDIA
SEBI/HO/CFD/CMD/CIR/2025/127

November 30, 2025

Circular on Business Responsibility and Sustainability Reporting (BRSR) Framework

The Securities and Exchange Board of India, in continuation of its circular SEBI/HO/CFD/CMD/CIR/2024/89 dated August 15, 2024, hereby issues the following framework for Business Responsibility and Sustainability Reporting (BRSR) by listed entities.

1. Applicability

1.1. BRSR reporting shall be mandatory for the top 1000 listed entities by market capitalization.
1.2. The reporting shall be on a comply-or-explain basis for the first two years.
1.3. Entities listed on the SME exchange shall submit a simplified BRSR Lite report.

2. Reporting Framework

The BRSR report shall cover the following nine principles of the National Guidelines on Responsible Business Conduct (NGRBC):

2.1. Principle 1 - Ethics and Transparency: Businesses shall conduct and govern themselves with integrity, in a manner that is ethical, transparent, and accountable.

2.2. Principle 2 - Product Sustainability: Businesses shall provide goods and services that are safe and contribute to sustainability throughout their life cycle.

2.3. Principle 3 - Employee Well-being: Businesses shall respect and promote the well-being of all employees, including those in their value chains.

2.4. Principle 4 - Stakeholder Engagement: Businesses shall respect the interests of and be responsive to all their stakeholders.

2.5. Principle 5 - Human Rights: Businesses shall respect and promote human rights.

2.6. Principle 6 - Environment: Businesses shall respect and make efforts to protect and restore the environment.

2.7. Principle 7 - Public Advocacy: Businesses shall engage in responsible public advocacy and refrain from anti-competitive conduct.

2.8. Principle 8 - Inclusive Growth: Businesses shall promote inclusive growth and equitable development.

2.9. Principle 9 - Customer Responsibility: Businesses shall provide value to their customers in a responsible manner.

3. ESG Disclosures

3.1. Listed entities shall disclose the following Environmental metrics:
   a) Greenhouse gas emissions (Scope 1, Scope 2, and Scope 3).
   b) Water withdrawal and consumption.
   c) Energy consumption and renewable energy usage.
   d) Waste generation and disposal methods.
   e) Environmental compliance and regulatory breaches.

3.2. Listed entities shall disclose the following Social metrics:
   a) Workforce diversity including gender, caste, and disability representation.
   b) Employee turnover and training hours.
   c) Occupational health and safety incidents.
   d) Community development expenditure.
   e) Human rights assessments and remediation.

3.3. Listed entities shall disclose the following Governance metrics:
   a) Board composition and independence.
   b) Executive compensation ratio.
   c) Whistleblower mechanism and cases reported.
   d) Anti-corruption policies and cases.
   e) Regulatory compliance and penalty history.

4. Assurance Requirements

4.1. All reported environmental data shall be subject to reasonable assurance by a certified third-party auditor.
4.2. Social and governance data shall be subject to limited assurance.
4.3. The assurance report shall be submitted along with the BRSR filing.

5. Digital Platform

All BRSR reports shall be filed through the SEBI BRSR digital platform available on the SEBI website. The filing window shall be open from April 1 to June 30 each year.

6. Timeline for Compliance

6.1. Top 500 entities by market capitalization: BRSR reporting mandatory from FY 2025-26.
6.2. Next 500 entities by market capitalization: BRSR reporting mandatory from FY 2026-27.
6.3. SME exchange listed entities: BRSR Lite from FY 2026-27.

7. Consequences of Non-Compliance

Non-compliance with BRSR filing requirements shall attract a penalty of Rs. 50,000 per day of delay, subject to a maximum of Rs. 50 lakh per entity per financial year.
